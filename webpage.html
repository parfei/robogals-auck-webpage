FACTS

WHAT IS IT?

COVID-19 is a new type of coronavirus that affects your lungs. The pandemic first began in Wuhan, China in December 2019. On the 30 January, 2020 the outbreak was named a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. Right now, there are no vaccines or treatments.

SYMPTOMS

The symptoms are similar to common illnesses such as colds or the flu. Here are a list of them:

  • dry cough
  • sore throat
  • shortness of breath
  • sneezing and runny nose
  • fever (at least 38 celcius)
If you have any of these symptoms, you can get tested here in NZ.

WHAT NEW ZEALAND IS DOING

New Zealand shut its borders to visitors, and declared a state of national emergency, escalating to level four. Everything was closed except for essential services. At first it was hard social distancing. However, we slowly started seeing results. On April 15, there were more recovered cases that active cases.

Now, we are at level one. More information is here.

HOW TO PROTECT YOURSELF AND OTHERS

The virus spreads person-to-person, through droplets containing the virus. It is spread through close contact of infected people. The droplets can be spread through talking, sneezing and coughing. They can even be on objects that we touch on a daily basis like on the bus. So the best way to protect yourself is to use basic hygiene.

WHAT MY COMMUNITY IS DOING

Red Cross delivers around 11,000 meals every week and medical supplies to vulnerable people. They are also sharing safety messages and translating them into 15 different languages.

Auckland Foundation has developed a community response fund that supports health and wellbeing of those affected by the NZ lockdown. These include mental health, those at risk of violence at home, and those who find it hard getting food or essential supplies.

There are a lot of university courses that are online for free to take.
